1. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This document outlines the Human Resources policies and procedures applicable to
all employees of TechCorp Industries. All employees are expected to familiarize
themselves with these policies and adhere to them at all times.

2. CODE OF CONDUCT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 Professional Behavior
All employees must conduct themselves in a professional manner during work hours
and at company-sponsored events. This includes treating colleagues, clients, and
partners with respect and dignity.

2.2 Anti-Harassment Policy
TechCorp Industries maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward any form of
harassment, including but not limited to:
- Sexual harassment
- Verbal or physical intimidation
- Discriminatory comments based on race, gender, religion, age, disability,
  or sexual orientation
- Cyberbullying through company communication channels

Any employee who experiences or witnesses harassment should report it immediately
to their manager or the HR department. All reports will be investigated promptly
and kept confidential to the extent possible.

2.3 Conflict of Interest
Employees must avoid situations that create or appear to create a conflict between
their personal interests and the interests of TechCorp Industries. Employees must
disclose any potential conflicts of interest to their supervisor or the HR
department. Examples include:
- Outside employment with a competitor
- Financial interest in a vendor or client company
- Family relationships with current or prospective employees

3. WORKING HOURS AND ATTENDANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Standard Working Hours
The standard working hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday, with
a one-hour lunch break. The total working week is 40 hours.

3.2 Flexible Working Arrangements
TechCorp Industries supports flexible working arrangements subject to manager
approval. Options include:
- Flextime: Adjusted start/end times within core hours (10 AM - 4 PM)
- Remote Work: Up to 3 days per week with manager approval
- Compressed Work Week: Four 10-hour days (available for specific roles)

3.3 Attendance Requirements
Employees are expected to maintain regular attendance. Unexcused absences may
result in disciplinary action. Employees must notify their supervisor before
their shift starts if they will be absent or late.

4. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 Salary Reviews
Salary reviews are conducted annually in April. Performance, market benchmarks,
and company financial performance are considered in determining salary adjustments.

4.2 Bonus Structure
- Annual Performance Bonus: Up to 15% of base salary based on individual and
  company performance
- Spot Bonuses: Up to $2,000 for exceptional contributions (manager-nominated)
- Referral Bonus: $3,000 for successful employee referrals (paid after 90-day
  retention period)

4.3 Benefits Package
All full-time employees are eligible for the following benefits after completing
their probationary period (90 days):
- Health Insurance: Comprehensive medical, dental, and vision coverage
- Retirement Plan: 401(k) with 4% company match
- Life Insurance: 2x annual salary coverage
- Wellness Allowance: $500 annual reimbursement for gym, fitness, or wellness
  activities
- Education Assistance: Up to $5,000 per year for approved courses and
  certifications

5. DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 Progressive Discipline
TechCorp Industries follows a progressive discipline approach:
Step 1: Verbal Warning (documented)
Step 2: Written Warning
Step 3: Final Written Warning / Suspension
Step 4: Termination

5.2 Grounds for Immediate Termination
The following offenses may result in immediate termination without progressive
discipline:
- Theft, fraud, or dishonesty
- Violence or threats of violence
- Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol during work hours
- Gross negligence resulting in significant damage or loss
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information

6. GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Employees who wish to raise a grievance should follow these steps:
Step 1: Discuss the issue with immediate supervisor
Step 2: If unresolved, escalate to the HR department in writing
Step 3: HR will investigate and respond within 10 business days
Step 4: If still unresolved, an appeal may be made to the VP of Human Resources

For further information, contact the HR Department at hr@techcorp.com.
